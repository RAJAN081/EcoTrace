Why Recycle E-Waste?

Electronic waste contains toxic materials like lead, mercury, and cadmium that can harm the environment and human health when improperly disposed of. Recycling e-waste recovers valuable materials and prevents pollution.

Environmental Impact

Only 20% of global e-waste is formally recycled. The rest ends up in landfills or is illegally dumped, causing soil and water contamination. Proper recycling can recover 95% of materials in electronics.

Valuable Resources

E-waste contains precious metals like gold, silver, and platinum. One ton of mobile phones contains more gold than one ton of gold ore! Recycling helps recover these valuable resources.
