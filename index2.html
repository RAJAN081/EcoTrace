Why Recycle E-Waste?

Environmental Protection

Recycling e-waste prevents harmful chemicals like lead, mercury, and cadmium from polluting our soil and water sources.

Resource Conservation

Electronics contain valuable materials like gold, silver, and copper that can be recovered and reused, reducing the need for mining.

Energy Savings

Recycling uses far less energy than extracting and processing raw materials, helping reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

E-Waste Awareness

The Growing E-Waste Problem

Global e-waste production is on track to reach 74 million metric tons by 2030, almost doubling the 2014 figures.

What Can Be Recycled?

Most electronics can be recycled including smartphones, computers, TVs, printers, and household appliances.

Proper Disposal Matters

When e-waste ends up in landfills, toxic substances can leach into soil and water, causing environmental damage.
